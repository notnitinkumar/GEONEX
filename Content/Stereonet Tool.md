## What is a Stereonet?
A stereonet (short for stereographic projection) is based on the Stereographic Projection and is widely used in Structural Geology.

It allows you to plot:

- Planes (e.g., bedding, faults)
- Lines (e.g., fold axes, lineations)

### Key Terms
1. **Strike**
    
    Strike is the direction of the horizontal line formed where a geological plane (like a rock layer or fault) intersects a horizontal surface.

    - It is measured as an angle (in degrees) from geographic north (0°–360°).
    - Represents the compass direction of the plane.

    Example: A strike of 090° means the plane runs east–west.

2. **Dip**

    Dip is the angle at which a plane inclines downward from the horizontal, measured perpendicular to the strike.

    - Measured in degrees from 0° (horizontal) to 90° (vertical).
    - Always includes a direction (e.g., N, SE, SW).

    Example: A dip of 30° SE means the plane is inclined 30° downward toward the southeast. 

**Together Strike and Dip**

Strike and dip together describe the orientation of a plane in 3D space, which is fundamental in Structural Geology.

Example:

- Strike: 045°
- Dip: 30° SE

This means the plane trends northeast–southwest and dips 30° toward the southeast.

3. **Trend**

    Trend is the direction of a line on a horizontal plane, measured as an angle (in degrees) clockwise from geographic north.

    - It tells you which way the line is pointing.
    - Expressed from 0° to 360°.

    Example: A line pointing northeast may have a trend of 045°.

4. **Plunge**

    Plunge is the angle at which a line dips downward from the horizontal plane.

    - It tells you how steeply the line is inclined.
    - Measured in degrees from 0° (horizontal) to 90° (vertical).

    Example: A line dipping moderately downward may have a plunge of 30°.

**Together (Trend + Plunge)**

Trend and plunge together fully describe the orientation of a line in 3D space, which is essential in Structural Geology.

Example:

- Trend: 120°
- Plunge: 25°

This means the line points southeast and dips downward at 25°.

## Steps to Use a 2D Stereonet Plotter

1. **Select Plot Type** 

    Choose from the Plane and Line.

2. **Enter Orientation Data**

    Input your geological data:

    - Strike & Dip (for planes)
    - Trend & Plunge (for lines)

    Example:

    - Strike: 045°
    - Dip: 30°

3. **Enter Label & Choose Color (Optional)**

    Enter label for a short information about the data and select color for the plot.

4. **Select a file (Other)**

    You can select a file in the csv/xlsx format with column enteries strike, dip, trend, plunge, label to automatically plot data.
